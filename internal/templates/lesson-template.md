# Horizon Learning Lesson Template

Version: 1.0

---

# Purpose

This template defines the standard structure used for every Horizon Learning lesson.

The goal is consistency.

Learners should always know what to expect.

---

# Learning Objectives

Use action-oriented objectives.

Good examples:

- Explain...
- Identify...
- Describe...
- Compare...
- Demonstrate...

Avoid:

- Learn...
- Understand...

Objectives should be measurable whenever possible.

Aim for 3–6 objectives.

---

# Goal

One short paragraph.

Answer:

"What is the learner trying to accomplish?"

---

# Why This Matters

Answer:

"Why should the learner care?"

Connect the lesson to real-world use.

---

# Plain-English Explanation

Introduce the concept in everyday language.

Avoid technical jargon until it has been explained.

Use analogies whenever they improve understanding.

---

# New Terms

List important terminology introduced in the lesson.

Define each term clearly.

---

# Main Lesson

Present the primary teaching content.

Prefer:

- Short paragraphs
- Bullet lists
- Diagrams
- Examples
- Tables

Avoid large blocks of text.

---

# Try It Yourself

Every lesson should include practical work.

Good examples:

- Observe
- Practice
- Experiment
- Create
- Draw
- Document

The activity should reinforce the lesson.

---

# Reflection Questions

Encourage learners to think.

Examples:

- Why...
- What surprised you?
- How would you explain this?
- What would happen if...?

Reflection is preferred over memorization.

---

# Common Mistakes

Identify beginner mistakes.

Explain why they happen.

Explain how to avoid them.

---

# Troubleshooting

Help learners recover from common problems.

Assume mistakes are normal.

Keep the tone encouraging.

---

# Stop and Check

Provide a checklist.

Example:

- [ ] I completed the exercise.
- [ ] I understand the concept.
- [ ] I can explain it to someone else.

---

# Lesson Summary

Review the major ideas.

Prefer bullet points.

Avoid introducing new information.

---

# What To Do Next

Point to the next lesson.

Explain why it naturally follows.

---

# Writing Standards

Lessons should be:

- Beginner friendly
- Technically accurate
- Mobile friendly
- Printable
- Plain English
- Encouraging
- Ethical
- Practical

---

# Required Review

Before publishing, verify:

Technical Review

- Commands tested
- Facts verified
- Links checked

Educational Review

- No unexplained jargon
- Reflection included
- Practice included
- Logical flow

Publishing Review

- Metadata complete
- Mobile friendly
- PDF friendly
- Navigation updated

---

# Admonition Guidelines

Use Material for MkDocs admonitions consistently.

!!! note

    Additional explanation.

!!! tip

    Helpful advice.

!!! warning

    Potential problems or safety concerns.

!!! success

    Milestones or completed tasks.

!!! info

    Interesting background information.

Do not overuse admonitions.

Use them to improve readability, not to decorate the page.
