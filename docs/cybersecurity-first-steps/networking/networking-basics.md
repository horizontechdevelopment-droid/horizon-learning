# Networking Basics

## Goal

Understand the basic parts of a home network.

## Plain-English explanation

A network is a group of devices that can communicate with each other.

Your home network may include:

- Phone
- Laptop
- Desktop computer
- Router
- Smart TV
- Printer
- Game console
- Security camera
- Smart home devices

Cybersecurity often starts with understanding what is connected and how it communicates.

## New terms

- **Network:** A group of connected devices.
- **Router:** The device that connects your home devices to each other and to the internet.
- **Modem:** The device that connects your home to your internet provider.
- **Device:** Anything connected to the network.
- **IP address:** A number used to identify a device.
- **Port:** A numbered communication doorway.
- **Service:** A program that listens for network connections.

## Router vs modem

A modem connects your home to your internet provider.

A router connects your home devices together and gives them access to the internet.

Sometimes the modem and router are built into the same box.

## Local network vs internet

Your local network is inside your home.

The internet is outside your home.

A laptop talking to your router is local network traffic.

A laptop opening a website is internet traffic.

## Why this matters

Before using security tools, you need to know what you are testing.

Scanning your own router is different from scanning a random website.

This guide only uses tools on systems and networks you own or have permission to test.

## Safe beginner task

Find your network information.

In Kali or Linux, run:

    ip addr

Look for an address that starts with something like:

    192.168.
    10.
    172.16.

These are common private network ranges.
