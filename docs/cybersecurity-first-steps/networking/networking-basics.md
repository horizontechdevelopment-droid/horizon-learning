## Goal

Understand the basic parts of a home network.

## Why This Matters

Cybersecurity depends heavily on networking.

Before you can understand tools like Nmap or Wireshark, you need to understand what devices are communicating and where that communication is going.

## Plain-English Explanation

A network is a group of devices that can communicate with each other.

Your home network may include:

- Phone
- Laptop
- Desktop computer
- Tablet
- Smart TV
- Printer
- Game console
- Router
- Smart speaker
- Security camera
- Doorbell camera

When these devices connect to the same Wi-Fi or wired network, they are part of your local network.

## New Terms

- **Network:** A group of connected devices.
- **Device:** Anything connected to the network.
- **Router:** The device that connects your home devices to each other and usually to the internet.
- **Modem:** The device that connects your home to your internet provider.
- **LAN:** Local Area Network. Your local home network.
- **WAN:** Wide Area Network. A larger network, such as the internet.
- **Internet:** A global network of networks.
- **Traffic:** Data moving across a network.

## The Basic Idea

A network lets devices talk.

For example:

- Your phone talks to your router.
- Your laptop talks to your printer.
- Your smart TV talks to streaming services.
- Your browser talks to websites.
- Your game console talks to online game servers.

The important idea is simple:

Devices send requests, and other devices or servers respond.

## Your Home Network

A simple home network may look like this:

    Phone
      |
    Laptop
      |
    Smart TV
      |
    Router
      |
    Internet

The router is usually the center of the home network.

Most devices connect to the router through Wi-Fi or Ethernet.

## Router vs Modem

These are often confused.

A modem connects your home to your internet provider.

A router connects your home devices together and gives them access to the internet.

Sometimes the modem and router are combined into one box.

## Local Network vs Internet

Your local network is inside your home.

The internet is outside your home.

Example local network communication:

- Your laptop prints to your printer.
- Your phone casts video to your TV.
- Your computer opens your router settings page.

Example internet communication:

- Your laptop opens a website.
- Your phone checks email.
- Your TV streams a movie.

## Why Cybersecurity Cares About Networks

Cybersecurity often starts with questions like:

- What devices are connected?
- What are they allowed to access?
- What services are running?
- Is anything exposed that should not be?
- Is unknown traffic happening?
- Are weak settings being used?

To answer those questions, you need basic networking knowledge.

Examples:

## Common Mistakes

- Thinking Wi-Fi and internet are the same thing
- Thinking the router and modem are always separate devices
- Forgetting that smart devices are computers too
- Assuming every unknown device is dangerous
- Jumping into scanning before understanding the network

## Troubleshooting

If you are not sure what a device is, check:

- Device name
- Manufacturer name
- Location in the house
- Whether someone in the home recognizes it
- Router connected-device list

Do not panic if a name looks strange.

Many devices show manufacturer names instead of friendly names.

## Summary

A network is a group of devices that can communicate.

Your home network is your local network.

Your router connects your devices together and usually connects them to the internet.

Networking matters because cybersecurity tools often study devices, traffic, services, and communication.
